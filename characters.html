Main Characters

Keita Ali
The protagonist. A gifted runner and refugee. Keita represents resilience and survival.

Keita Ali is the protagonist of The Illegal. He is a refugee and an elite long distance runner who is forced to flee his home country after violence and political corruption threaten his life. Throughout the novel, Keita is constantly on the run, not just in races but from authorities who label him illegal. His character represents resilience and survival because he continues to push forward despite fear, poverty, and injustice. Running becomes both his talent and his way to stay alive.

Charity Ali
Keita younger sister. She represents innocence and motivation.

Charity Ali is Keita younger sister. She represents innocence and hope in the novel. Charity depends on Keita for protection and support, which motivates many of his decisions. Her vulnerability reminds the reader of what is at stake if Keita fails. Charity symbolizes the innocent people who suffer the most during political violence and forced migration.

Viola Hill
A journalist who helps Keita while investigating injustice in Freedom State.

Viola Hill is a journalist who helps Keita while investigating injustice in Freedom State. She risks her career and safety to expose the truth about how undocumented migrants are treated. Viola represents moral courage and justice because she chooses to do what is right even when it is dangerous. Her role shows the importance of speaking out against unfair systems and standing up for those who cannot protect themselves.

Anton Hamm
A corrupt sports agent who exploits desperate athletes.

Anton Hamm Is a corrupt sports agent who takes advantage of Keita talent as a runner. He promises Keita success and safety but instead treats him like a product to make money. Hamm uses Keita undocumented status to control him and offers no real protection or support. His character represents greed and exploitation and shows how vulnerable refugees can be abused by individuals, not just governments.

Character Importance

Together, these characters show how individuals are shaped by larger systems. Keita and Charity represent survival, vulnerability, and the human cost of political violence. Anton Hamm represents exploitation and greed, showing how powerful individuals take advantage of refugees desperation for personal gain. Viola Hill represents moral courage and justice, demonstrating how individuals can challenge unfair systems by speaking out and taking risks.
