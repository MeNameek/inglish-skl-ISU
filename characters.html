Main Characters

Keita Ali
The protagonist. A gifted runner and refugee. Keita represents resilience and survival.

Keita grows up surrounded by poverty and danger, forcing him to mature quickly. His talent for running becomes both his escape and his burden. Throughout the novel, Keita represents the strength required to survive in a world that treats refugees as disposable.

Charity Ali
Keita younger sister. She represents innocence and motivation.

Charity shows how refugee crises affect children and families. Her dependence on Keita adds emotional pressure and highlights vulnerability. She symbolizes hope but also the responsibility placed on refugees to protect loved ones.

Anton Hamm
A corrupt sports agent who exploits desperate athletes.

Hamm represents greed and exploitation. He views athletes as financial assets rather than people. His character exposes how powerful individuals profit from global inequality.

Rocco Calder
An immigration minister who enforces harsh refugee laws.

Calder represents political hypocrisy. He presents himself as moral while supporting policies that destroy lives. His character criticizes governments that value power over compassion.

Character Importance

Together, these characters show how individuals are shaped by larger systems. Keita and Charity show survival and vulnerability. Hamm and Calder reveal exploitation and abuse of authority.
